Frequently Asked Questions

Is MindCare a substitute for professional medical advice?

No. MindCare is designed to be a supportive tool, but it is not a substitute for professional diagnosis or treatment. For serious conditions, always consult with a qualified mental health professional or your doctor.

How is my data kept private?

We use end-to-end encryption for all conversations and strictly adhere to privacy policies. Your personal information is never shared without your explicit consent. Our goal is to create a safe and confidential environment for all users.

How do I become a Peer Volunteer?

To become a Peer Volunteer, you must first register and complete our mandatory training program. Our team will then review your application and conduct a brief interview to ensure a good fit for the community.

How much does MindCare cost?

Access to peer support and all of our self-help resources, including journals and mindful media, is completely **free** for all students. There may be a small fee for professional counseling sessions, but these are often covered by your university partnership, making them highly affordable. You can view specific costs in your profile after logging in.
